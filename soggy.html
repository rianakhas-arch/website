Soggy Sandwiches

Praxis I Design Project

This project addressed the problem of sandwiches becoming soggy during storage due to moisture transfer. Our team designed and tested multiple concepts to improve freshness and usability, ultimately developing a layered container system.

Opportunity

Students often prepare sandwiches hours before eating them, leading to soggy bread and reduced food quality. We identified moisture transfer from ingredients as the primary cause and focused on preserving freshness while maintaining convenience.

Design Process

We developed five concepts including Pull-Out Layers, Skewer, and Parchment Separation. Each concept was evaluated using testing methods such as assembly time, moisture absorption, and spillage. We used comparison tools like Pugh charts and pairwise ranking to select the best design.

Final Design

The final concept was a pull-out layered container that separates ingredients until consumption. This approach minimized moisture transfer while remaining practical and easy to use.

Results

  • Assembly time: ~53 seconds
  • Minimal bread compression compared to alternatives
  • Best overall performance in final concept selection

Reflection

This project showed the importance of combining user needs with technical testing. It reinforced that strong design decisions come from structured comparison and real-world evaluation.
